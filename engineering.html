Engineering

Values

Compassionate design

I encourage engineers I work with to design for radical maintainability. This may mean using an existing pattern versus trying something new, spending an extra 5 minutes to choose the right name for something, or going the extra mile to document an interface. I've learned that a basic design that can be easily recognized and reconfigured when needed is more valuable than an elegant solution that doesn't make sense when I'm gone.

Support and collaboration

Fear, comparison, and competition are so entrentched in engineering culture that many developers confuse demonstrating (or signaling) intelligence with acheivement. The best solutions arise when teams of talented, motivated people feel safe to share what they know and admit what they don't. Pairing and mobbing are powerful tools in the hands of engineers who are not afraid of being exposed, or incentivized to collect personal wins.

Excellence as impact

While great engineering fundamentals never go out of style, I believe excellence in application development should look different in 2019 than it did in 2009, or 1999. I advocate for hiring and compensation systems that reward anticipation of users' needs and contribution to the business's mission in addition to technical mastery. With modern tooling and skillsets, product and engineering can and should operate as a tightly-integrated group rather than reluctant allies with overlapping goals.
